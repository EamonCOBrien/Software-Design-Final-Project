How to use our Product




FYI

Our code doesn't work on heroku perfectly yet and we believe it will take more skill and more time than we currently have to make our webapp quickly access and process the camera feed. As a result, all the instructions for downloading and using our program is detailed below.


What you need

If you've gotten this far without downloading the program from github, Congrats! We don't know how you got this far, but you should really go to github now, read the ReadMe and follow the instructions.


Now that you have the CVPaint downloaded you can start having fun. To get the most out of your CVPaint experience, you will need a magic wand. CVPaint's wand tracking uses color tracking, so as long as you have something with 2 different colors for the program to track (and you don't happen to be wearing one of those colors) you'll be fine. Your wand will only be limited by your imagination, but here are a few ideas to start you off:

  1. Two sticky notes back to back. You can use a pencil as a handle.
  2. A ping pong paddle
  3. Your phone (in a pinch)
  4. Magic Wand*

    5. *Recommended if you have acess to a 3D printer go to STL download here


How To Play

To start the program, you must click the rightmost tab labeled Start. You will then be directed to the calibration screen. Follow the instructions on the screen asking you to hold the first side of your wand in the circle. After 5 seconds the program will capture and save the color of whatever is in the circle at that time. Then do the same for the second side of your wand. This will determine which side of the wand is the selector, and which is the pen.

We'll talk about all the cool gadgets you can use.

After calibration, you can start exploring and drawing whatever you wish. The tools and gadgets on the top of your screen are as follows: Clear, pen, eraser, thickness, color, rectangle, circle, and recalibrate. To use each tool, you must first select it with the selector side of your wand. (side 2). Then you can flip the wand and use the pen tool (side 1).


Tools and Gadets

This is the clear button. When clicked, all of the drawings on the screen will be deleted.

This is the pen-tool button. When clicked (if the tool is currently eraser, rectangle, or circle), this button allows you to draw free-form lines again.

This is the erase button. It changes the drawing wand color to an eraser that will erase parts of lines as it is waved across them.

This is the thickness button. It brings up a menu of thicknesses that effect the line thickness and eraser size.




These are the thickness options. They appear when the thickness button is pressed. They represent the three possible line thickness and eraser sizing options. Wave the wand over the size of choice to continue with your drawing.


This is the color choice button. When selected it brings up the color slider.






This is the color slider. It appears when the color choice button is pressed. Wave the wand over this bar until the desired color appears in the box to it's right. Then carefully move the wand off of the color bar and click the colored check box.

This is the rectangle button. With it selected, the first time the program sees the pen side of your wand, it will start drawing a rectangle with the cursor's first position as the corner. The rectangle will end when you switch back to the select side, connecting the opposite corners.

This is the ellipse button. With it selected, the first time the program sees the pen side of your wand, it will start drawing an ellipse with the cursor's first location as the center. Turn the wand back to the select side when you are happy with the radius you've selected.

This is the recalibration button. If the wand isn't registering correctly, click this to rerun the initial calibration. Also consider moving to an area with less in the background.
